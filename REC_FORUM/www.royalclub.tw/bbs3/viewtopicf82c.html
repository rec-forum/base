Article 1: Kaohsiung to tax house hoarders

https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/ar ... 2003768443

Q1. In your opinion, how many houses that a person has called house hoarder? Do you agree with the government to tax house hoarders? Why or why not?

Q2. Why do Taiwan people love to have own house? Do you think it’s easy to buy a house in your city? Why or why not?

Q3. If you want to buy a house? What are your considerations? (Location, prices, neighbors, school, near the office…)

Q4. Do you have your own house? Can you share how did you achieve this goal? If you don’t have your own house and you want to buy a house, how will you plan your money and mortgage? How many years do you think you will make it?

Q5. What are the reasons that cause the housing is so unreachable? Do you think what government can do?


Article 2: Shoppers prefer credit cards: report

https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/ar ... 2003768502

Q1. How many credit cards do you have? What reasons make you apply for the credit card? (such like cash-back, free parking, free gift, friend’s recommendation…)

Q2. Where do you usually shop? (online shopping or shopping in the stores…) When you purchase something, do you prefer to pay by cash, credit card, check, debit card…? Why? What are the advantages and disadvantages of your payment methods?

Q3. Can you share your experience with paid by credit card? Is there anything that you need to aware of? (such like someone swipe your credit card, personal information was leaked…). Do you have any bad experience with using credit card?

Q4. Some people prefer to pay by credit card with installments? Have you ever tried it? Why or why not? Can you share your experience?

Q5. Do you think that paid by credit card is a good habit? How do you control yourself not spend over your budget? Do you have any experience of spending over your monthly income that you can’t pay the bill? How did you solve the problem?
