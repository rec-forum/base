April 8, 2021 (Thu.) Asia's first 'travel bubble' opens between Taiwan and Palau/Passenger train carrying 490 derails in Taiwan (Host: Julia)

Place: 丹堤咖啡, 台北市延平南路107號2樓 (MRT 西門站4號出口)
Agenda:
07:00-7:05 pm Opening Remarks
07:05-7:40 pm Session 1 Group Discussion
07:40-8:00 pm Presentation (At least two members of each table are invited, 2 mins / each member)
8:00-8:05 pm Break
8:05-8:40 pm Session 2 discussion
8:40-9:00 pm Presentation

Session 1. Asia's first 'travel bubble' opens between Taiwan and Palau

https://edition.cnn.com/travel/article/ ... index.html

Questions:
1. How do you like the “travel bubble”? Are you interested in trying it now or waiting for the end of Covid-19 to travel abroad?
2. Besides Palau, what other countries do you expect to open a reciprocal travel lane with Taiwan? Why?
3. What do you think about the restrictions on the travel bubble: arrive five and a half hours early to test Covid-19 on the airport, stick to strict itineraries, and wear a mask in public?
4. Do you think the package is too expensive or worth the cost?

Session 2. Passenger train carrying 490 derails in Taiwan, killing at least 50 and injuring dozens

https://edition.cnn.com/2021/04/01/asia ... index.html

Questions:
1. What are the most dangerous ways of transportation? Why?
2. “Most vehicle crashes, and the majority of all transportation-related injuries and fatalities, are caused by human error.” Do you agree on the saying? Why or why not?
3. To what extent, do you agree that life is unpredictable? What are your ways to reduce or respond to uncertainty of life?
4. Living in the present/stopping worrying about tomorrow, and cherishing/being grateful to the people around us usually come out of our mind when a serious disaster occurs. Do you practice these ideas in your daily life? If yes, please provide us with some examples.
