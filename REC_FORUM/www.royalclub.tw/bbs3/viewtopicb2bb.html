Session I: The smell of coffee

Please watch the following video to learn about “the smell of coffee” from BBC 6 minute English:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5R3WdBE1-JM

Questions:
1. If you watched the video, please share what you learned from it, such as new ideas, vocabularies, and phrases?

2. Do you like drinking coffee? If yes, how do you make it, by purchasing it from a shop, or brewing it by yourself? If you don’t like coffee, what other kind of beverage do you like? And why?

3. Do you think the “the smell of coffee” is important? What make good coffee(or the beverage you like) to you?

4. What does it mean to have “good taste”? Drinking Starbucks coffee means having better taste than having 7-11 coffee? Or using iPhone instead of other brands? Going to see an opera instead of a rap/hip hop concert? Collecting real artworks instead of IKEA paintings? Wearing Prada instead of NET? What does it mean when someone says someone has good taste?



Session II: Should we have a bucket list?
Please watch the following video to learn about “Should we have a bucket list?” from BBC 6 minute English:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nb7wVCJ68YY


Questions:
1. If you watched the video, please share what you learned from it, such as new ideas, vocabularies, and phrases?

2. What are the pros and cons to have a bucket list?

3. Are you a spontaneous or organized person? What reasons made you such kind of person? Do you like yourself now? Why or why not?

4. Do you have a bucket list? If yes, what are the items on your list and how many of them have been completed? If no, do you have any goals in your life?
