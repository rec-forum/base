Session I:
Colour affects us

https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/e ... /ep-210902
Introduction
Do red and yellow make you happy? Are you sad in a environment with a lot of blue? This is the programme where Neil and Georgina hope to add some colour to your life by discussing how colour can make you feel. And they teach you useful vocabulary too.
This week's question
Do you know what the splitting of white light into its different colours is called?
a) dispersion
b) reflection
c) refraction
Listen to the programme to find out the answer.
Vocabulary
green with envy
the feeling someone has who wishes they had what someone else has
wavelength
distance between two waves of sound or light that are next to each other
govern
control or influence
sanctuary
peaceful, relaxing or safe place
stimulation
feeling of being excited, interested or enthused by something
pale
colour that lacks intensity

Georgina

It’s the way that we take in the wavelengths of light because colour is wavelengths of light, and it’s how that comes in through our eye, and then it goes into the part of our brain called the hypothalamus, which governs our sleeping patterns, our hormones, our behaviours, our appetite – it governs everything and so different colours and different frequencies or different wavelengths of light, we have different responses and different reactions to them.

Neil

Georgina
Homeware and furnishing manufacturers offer a whole spectrum – or range – of colours to choose to suit everyone’s taste, and mood. But what I have found since the first lockdown is a lot of people, because they’re not getting that outside stimulation, they’re actually putting a lot of brighter colours in their home because they’re trying to bring in that feeling that they would have got when they were out – that excitement and that buzz.

Session II:
Taiwan to lower Level 3 alert on July 27
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5aA6aYB8Rtw

As widely expected, the government has announced that Taiwan''s Level 3 COVID alert will be lowered starting next Tuesday. Under the new Level 2 alert, limits on gatherings will be relaxed to 50 people indoors and 100 outdoors. Wedding banquets will be allowed, but religious processions will stay banned. Let''s hear from the Central Epidemic Command Center.

Chen Shih-chung
CECC
We’re announcing a state of Level 2 for the period of July 27 to Aug. 9. We all must exercise heightened vigilance. We do not rule out raising the alert at any time. After the COVID alert is lowered to Level 2, masks will remain mandatory outdoors at all times, except when eating and drinking. Registration for contact tracing must be implemented, and social distancing must be maintained. Crowd control must be implemented, with at least 2.25 square meters allotted indoors for each person, and 1 square meter allotted outdoors. Gatherings are capped at 50 people indoors and 100 people outdoors. If the limit must be exceeded, there must be an epidemic prevention plan in place. Regarding dining in at restaurants, we will maintain the rules announced previously. Businesses that must remain closed, are leisure and entertainment venues, including karaoke parlors and arcades. They also include education-related centers such as commercial study centers, as well as swimming pools, and other such places.

Although the CECC has allowed dining in since July 13, the final say has been at the discretion of local governments. So far, most local governments have decided to maintain bans on dining in. But on Friday, the Taoyuan City government said it would reopen restaurants next week. Taichung, Tainan, and Kaohsiung said they would launch a review before announcing a decision. Taipei and New Taipei said they''ll keep their bans on dining in for at least another week after July 27. Also on Friday, the CECC announced 23 local cases, 17 from the greater Taipei area, and 2 COVID-related deaths.
