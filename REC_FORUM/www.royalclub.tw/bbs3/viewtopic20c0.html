Session I : Millions Displaced in Turkey and Syria after Earthquake
https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/ ... earthquake

Question
1. What is your experience in earthquakes? What did you do then?
2. Do you always get a survival pack prepared just in case of serious quakes? What are normally required in a survival pack?
3. It’s hard to imagine being displaced after a disaster. What will you most likely do if that happens to you and makes you homeless?
4. Any movie about earthquakes you’ve ever watched? Please introduce it.
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Session II: 8-year-old Indian Diamond Heiress Who Became a Nun
https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-india-64327940

Question

1. Do you believe a child or teenager can decide for his/her own life? Why?
2. Some religion hold it sacred to “baptize” a kid even infant at very young age. Do you agree with that?
3. What will you do in case your kids show strong interest in spiritual life and even about to renounce from the mundane world?
4. Sometimes what is allowed in religious world is not allowed in legal world (and vice versa). For example free speech against some religion may be considered as blasphemy. How should we show our respect to a religion or even to atheists?
