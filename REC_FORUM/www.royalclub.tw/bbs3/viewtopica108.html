SESSION1:
What would you do? He's Not the Father, Would You Tell?
The hypothetical situation is that, you're sitting at a restaurant, and you can't help but overhear a conversation from the next table. A young woman excited about her pregnancy. she’s considering to tell her boyfriend who think he's the father but really isn't. Worse yet, after a discussion with her friend, the expected mom still has no plans to tell her boyfriend the truth, because she thinks he will be the better option for the husband. What would you do if you were the fly on the wall?
Please find the video from the link below,
https://tw.voicetube.com/videos/33733

Questions:
1. What would you do if you overheard it from the next table, will you save the innocent boy on the spot?
2. “He's not the Father”, if you were the friend of both the boy and girl, what will you do?
3. Is the pregnant girl so selfish or it can be one of the cures for her well-being? If you were her, are there any options you could choose for?
4. What if you were her parent, what would you do?

SESSION2:
Who are better, men or women?
I think it’s a wonderful question. It is only juvenile to people who are still possessed by the Sixties myth of androgyny. About fifty years ago we decided that if we all just hold hands, close our eyes and believe hard enough, the fundamental differences between men and women would disappear and we would all be the same, and on the heavenly road to equality. The selfish motivations for this ploy do not concern us here. But the fact remains that men and women are deeply and fundamentally different. So this Quora question elicits a much more important one: better at what? Here is an arbitrary list:
War: men are better. Before we had lawyers men settled power struggles through combat. This is why we still speak of arms and armies. Men are better killers, though technology is rapidly changing this edge.
Sports: men definitely have the edge. These games were designed to display physical prowess used in combat. This is why we rarely allow men and women to compete against each other. It wouldn’t be fair due to undeniable innate differences.
Social Skills: I believe that on average women are just naturally more adept arbitrary at reading social situations, and understanding relationships between people.
Compassion: no contest. Women are naturally more compassionate.
Language skills: countless studies have shown that girls, on average, learn languages better, more completely than boys
Math skills: boys have an innate advantage in math
As Physicians: probably women
As Engineers: probably men
As Lawyers: take yer pick
As Dental Hygienists: probably women
As Auto Mechanics: probably men
As Food Servers: take yer pick
Heavy lifting: men are better
Child birth and breast feeding: definitely women
Parallel parking: men – but pretty soon we’ll all have self-driving cars so it won’t matter.
So which gender is better? that is subjective. It has to do with our entire spectrum of values. We’d like to be fair, and not discriminate on account of gender. But what if our values actually do favor one gender’s innate traits over the other? In such circumstances I can only speak for myself: women are better – better on average mind you - and not by much - but definitely the superior sex.

Questions:
1. Who are better, men or women?
2. What was the best part of being a man or woman you’ve taken? how about the worst one you’ve suffered?
3. If you could argue with god choosing any gender you want to be, which one would you prefer?
4. Which one is good at language skills, women or men?
