10/10(Sat.)14 benefits of solo travel/The secret to living longer may be your social life(Host:Cammy)

Session I:
14 benefits of solo travel
https://www.insightguides.com/inspire-m ... olo-travel
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rDS8UMKhIJw
These days, an increasing number of modern explorers are taking vacations by themselves. As the solo travel movement goes from strength to strength, we are highlighting some of the positive aspects of taking a trip on your own. There's nothing quite like discovering a new place on your own – taking it all in without anyone but yourself. Anyone who's done it can tell you that the benefits of solo travel are endless. Still, that's not stopping us from highlighting 14 of the most compelling perks of traveling alone.
Extended reading:
Solo travel, couple travel, and travel with friends all have their pros and cons and it can be tough to know which will suit you best. This article outlines the advantages and disadvantages of hitting the road on your own for the first time.
https://www.tripsavvy.com/pros-and-cons ... el-3150761

Questions:
Q1: Have you ever thought about travelling alone? What kinds of questions
have you been worried and scary when you traveling alone? Please
share your comments or experiences and how to overcome it?
Q2: What was your favorite destination you traveled solo if your time was
available? Please introduce the special culture, history, local foods,
language and scenery of this place with table members.
Q3: In this article mentioned” You'll probably improve your language skills –
When you're traveling alone, on the other hand, you're forced to
constantly practice the new language.” Do you agree or not? Why? Also,
please share your learning new language experience abroad or
homestay with foreigners
Q4: Please summarize the pros and cons of solo travel from above articles,
video and table discussion

Session II:
The secret to living longer may be your social life
https://www.ted.com/talks/susan_pinker_ ... ocial_life
Questions:
Q1: Referring to this article, the Italian island of Sardinia has more than six times as many centenarians as the mainland and ten times as many as North America. Why? According to the psychologist Susan Pinker, it's not a sunny disposition or a low-fat, gluten-free diet that keeps the islanders healthy - it's their emphasis on close personal relationships and face-to-face interactions.
Do you agree with above scientific findings or not? Please provide your comments.
Q2: What kind of staying alive factor can reduce our chances of dying the most? Why? Ex: Exercise, Quit Smoking, Lean vs. Overweight, Flu vaccine, Close relationship, social integration…
Q3: Nowadays, people communicate more online than face-to-face with their smartphones, computers and TV sets as they progress in civilization and technology. How to improve social relationship in person and practice face-to-face interaction at workplace or leisure time?
Q4: What’s your best learning from this lecture? How will you apply it to your future life?
