Session I: My philosophy for a happy life

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=36m1o-t ... e=youtu.be
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Berns

Just before his passing on January 10, 2014, Sam Berns was a Junior at Foxboro High School in Foxboro, Massachusetts, where he achieved highest honors and was a percussion section leader in the high school marching band. He also achieved the rank of Eagle Scout in the Boy Scouts of America. Sam was diagnosed with Progeria, a rare, rapid aging disease, at the age of 2. Progeria, a rare disease, the premature aging disease include: tight skin, lack of weight gain, stunted growth, and heart disease. That affects an estimated 200 to 250 children worldwide.
He is featured in the Emmy award-winning documentary Life According to Sam, which premiered on HBO on October 21, 2013, 2 days before his 17th birthday.

Questions:
Q1: This film mentioned that Sam got Progeria then John Hamilton asked Sam the question: “What is the most important thing that people should know about you?” And Sam’s answer was simply that I have a very happy life. Also, most of his time is spent thinking about things that have nothing to do with Progeria at all.
What do think about Sam’s philosophy? At the same time, share what you learned about the hard challenges of people with rare diseases
Q2: Referring to Sam’s speech, “the first aspect to his philosophy is that I’m okay with what I ultimately can’t do because there is so much I can do” … what do think his attitude? Also, please share your own experience about this philosophy.
Q3: Sam’s second philosophy is that I surround myself with people I want to be with, people of high quality. Do you agree or not? What kinds of people always support you throughout your life?
Q4: What methods do you often use to enable you to move forward quickly on bad day? Please share your tips or experience with table members
Q5: What’s your best learning from this lecture? How will you apply it to your future life?

Session II: Learning a language? Speak it like you’re playing a video game

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ge7c7ot ... ture=share

Questions:
Q1: How many years have you studied English? What bottlenecks have you encountered in the process of learning English? How did you overcome it?
Q2: From this TED video, the speaker, Marianna Pascal, shows how the secret to speaking a new language with confidence is all about attitude, not ability. Please share your comments or experiences.
Q3: Referring to this speech, " English is not an art to be mastered, it's just a tool to use to get results " Do you agree or not? Why?
Q4: Today is the end of this year. What are your expectations for learning English in the new year? How will you achieve it?
