Session I - How do your morals compare?

https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-50487054

Discussion
Q1: What did you get in the moral quiz? Does a higher score mean higher morality?
Q2: What are the best examples of actions that are moral, even uplifting, but illegal?
Q3: Can morality be rated or judged?
Q4: Is morality subjective or objective? Why?


Session II - When do kids start to care about other people's opinions?

https://www.ted.com/talks/sara_valencia_botto_when_do_kids_start_to_care_about_other_people_s_opinions

Discussion
Q1: How do you teach kids about other people's opinions?
Q2: How have people learned to stop caring about other’s opinions?
Q3: Do you care about other people's opinions of you?
Q4: How do you tactfully deal with people's opinions about you?
