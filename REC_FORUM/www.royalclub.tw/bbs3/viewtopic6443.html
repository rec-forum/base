Session I
What Causes Hunger? It's Not What You Think

https://www.wfpusa.org/drivers-of-hunger/

Questions:
1. “Conflict is the first driver of hunger in the world.”
Do you agree with this expression? Are there any episodes or news to share?

2. “women are more likely than men to suffer from hunger and malnourishment. That’s because in many cases, women eat last and least.”
Do you know the possible reasons why women eat last and least in some countries?

3. “In high-income countries, 40 percent of food is wasted.”
How often do you check your refrigerator? Is your refrigerator always full? Are you overbought? How do you manage your food purchases without wasting food?

4. Do you buy expiring food at the supermarket? If not, why?
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Session II
Spain plans to control food waste – and all restaurants must offer ‘doggy bags’

https://www.spainenglish.com/2022/06/08 ... oggy-bags/

Questions:
1. How do you judge this new draft bill? Can it effectively control food waste?

2. Do you use dog bags to take leftover food home at a restaurant/wedding reception? Share your experience.

3. Do you often cook? What do you do with leftovers? Do you usually eat leftovers at your next meal?

4. Many people like to prepare/order so many dishes at meals or parties. Is it related to one's habits/culture?
