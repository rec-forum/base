Session 1: Men Spend More Time Gossiping Than Women, Says New Study
https://kasatintin.com/2020/02/10/men-s ... new-study/

The study which was conducted by global research company Onepoll.com found that on the average, men spend 76 minutes a day gossiping with their friends or work colleagues, compared to just 52 minutes for women.
The study suggested that men have favourite topics they chat about which include the antics of drunken friends, old school friends, and the most attractive girl at work.

Questions:
1. Do you gossip? What’s your favourite Gossip topics? What’s the hottest celebrity gossip recently?
2. Why do most people love to gossip? Do men really more inclined to gossip than women?
3. Is gossip good for your social and mental health?
4. What is the difference between harmful and harmless gossip? What kind of gossip can become problematic?
5. Does the "prosocial" gossip have the function of warning others about untrustworthy or dishonest people?


Session 2: How to Make a Conversation : 7 Ways to Make Conversation with Anyone
https://www.imnepal.com/ways-make-conversation-anyone/

“We mustn’t speak to strangers.” Malavika Varadan, challenges this societal norm, by presenting 7 ways to make conversation with anyone.
https://tw.video.search.yahoo.com/searc ... tion=click

Questions:
1. Is it easy for you to make a conversation with anyone?
2. How to be a good conversationalist?
3. What are the great conversation starters for any occasion?
4. How to keep a conversation going and never run out of things to say?
5. When you trapped in a conversation that you didn’t want to be in. How will you end a conversation politely?
