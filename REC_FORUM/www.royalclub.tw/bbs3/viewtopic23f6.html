Session I: Why Is Everyone So Bad At Giving Gifts?
Do you often receive an unwelcome gift? According to the video, around 70 billion dollars’ worth of presents are returned every year in the U.S. In this video, The Atlantic writer Derek Thompson explains why many presents amount to what economists call “deadweight loss”: the company wasted time making it, the giver wasted time buying it, and the receiver wasted time returning it.
So, how do we design a gift-giving formula that strikes the right balance between efficient, practical, and sentimental? Please watch the following video:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q9UUu2JBQB0

The Tips are summarized as follows,
1. Stop trying to make your gifts so delightful.
2. When in doubt, give cash.
3. Just give people what they ask for.

Session II: Japanese life expectancy sees 1st decline in 10 years amid pandemic

Life expectancy in Japan has fallen for the first time in 10 years, according to data released by the Japanese Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare.
The data shows an average life expectancy of 87.57 years for women and 81.47 years for men in 2021. The figures are a decrease from record highs of 87.71 years for women and 81.56 years for men in 2020.
Life expectancy in Japan had been increasing every year since 2011 — which was the last time life expectancy fell. That year, the effects of the strongest earthquake recorded in the country's history, and the tsunami that followed, left more than 18,000 people dead or missing.
As reported by The Japan Times, the ministry says the recent decrease is an effect of COVID-19, adding that the virus was the reason for lower figures for most men.
However, Japan has previously set global records for life expectancy. A 2016 study published in BMC Research Notes found that Japan had the longest life expectancy in the world in 2013, and noted that it was the first to achieve an average male life expectancy of more than 80 years.
According to World Population Review, Hong Kong currently has the highest life expectancy in the world, at 87.8 years for women and 82 years for men. Japan is second, followed by Macau, which has a life expectancy of 87.2 years for women and 81.3 years for men.
Based on data from the UN, average global life expectancy was 72.8 years in 2019, and is expected to increase to 77.2 years in 2050.
