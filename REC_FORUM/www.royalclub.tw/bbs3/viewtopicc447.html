Session 1: Arguments for and against capital punishment

https://www.britannica.com/topic/capita ... punishment
https://onyourpsy.com/20200416-1/

Questions
1. Are you pro or anti capital punishment (death penalty)? What are the reasons?
2. There is an abolitionist group in R.O.C called Taiwan Alliance to End the Death Penalty (TAEDP). Every time when serious criminal cases happened, the public clamor for death penalty and then TAEDP will assert their attitude against it. Do you know their claims?
3. There is a point of view in the article about utilitarian: “the death penalty is not a more effective deterrent than the alternative sanction of life or long-term imprisonment”. Or simply “killing can’t prevent killing （以殺不能止殺）”. Do you agree?
4. Usually the justice on court rules out death penalty to the defendant based on reason called “possibility of edification （教化）”. Here I attach a weblink in Chinese discussing about edification. Please share your thoughts about the possibility of edification.



Session 2: 10 Tips for Enjoying Your Class Reunion

https://guideposts.org/positive-living/ ... s-reunion/

Questions:
1. Did you join a class reunion recently? When and what was it? Did you enjoy? If you never join a class reunion, why?
2. What could daunt you or embarrass you in a class reunion? Can you have a new eye and forgiving heart toward your old classmates?
3. Do you compare yourself to your classmates about your circumstances? What about if you feel inferior/superior?
4. In 30 years or more the look of a person could vary a lot. Anything interesting? What do you usually do to recall each other about the past?
