Session 1: Man accidentally buys two identical lottery tickets, wins two jackpots
https://www.upi.com/Odd_News/2023/02/27 ... 677527587/
1. What do you think are the odds of winning the lottery, and how does this man's story change your perception of those odds?
2. If you won a large sum of money, what would you do with it? Do you agree with this man's decision to put some of it towards his son's education and save the rest?
3. Have you ever won a prize or lottery before? If so, what did you do with the winnings?
4. Do you think it was a coincidence that the man won two jackpots with the same set of numbers, or do you believe there is a strategy to increasing your chances of winning the lottery?
5. Some people believe that winning the lottery brings more problems than benefits. Do you agree or disagree with this sentiment, and why?

Session 2: Daily walk prevents one in 10 early deaths - study
https://www.bbc.com/news/health-64790527
1. How do you currently incorporate physical activity into your daily routine?
2. What are some barriers that prevent you from meeting the recommended amount of weekly physical activity?
3. What types of physical activities do you enjoy doing, and how do they benefit your overall health?
4. How can workplaces and communities encourage more physical activity among their members?
5. How do you prioritize physical activity in your busy schedule and make time for it?
