6:30-7:15 pm Free talk

7:15-7:50 pm Session I discussion
7:50-8:15 pm Session I Presentation (At least two members of each table are invited, 2 mins / each member)

8:15-8:20 pm Halftime activity: Lucky draw and group photo

8:20-8:45 pm Session II discussion
8:45-9:00 Session II Presentation


Session I: Let's make REC great again

1. Background
REC (Royal English Club) was founded by William Lu in 1999, and had been the most popular English club in Taipei for more than 10 years. However, REC has not been as popular as before for a while. In order to make REC great again, under the leadership of Founder William Lu and President Julia Ho, the first ever questionnaire survey was conducted between Jan. 8 and Jan. 20, 2021. The purpose of this survey is to more scientifically and objectively find ways to improve REC based on the collected opinions.

With members’ and friends’ kind support, a total of 55 responses are received, and 27 of them are from females. The respondents cover all ages, but 41-65 accounts for 56.3%. Around 89% are members. 50% are regular workers, but 28% are retired. Although the respondents are limited and incomprehensive, they generally reflect the current status of REC participation. The survey result may still provide helpful findings/suggestions for improvement.

Please look at the PDF file in the following link for the statistical results, findings and suggestions based on the 55 responses.
https://drive.google.com/file/d/1x4lzWX ... sp=sharing

In order to facilitate meeting discussion, the key results and suggestions are summarized as follows:

2. What most participants love about REC?
2.1. Top three Reasons for members to keep coming:
2.1.1. Learning/sharing new knowledge through meeting topics;
2.1.2. Promoting/maintaining English Level without specific purpose;
2.1.3. Making friends with diverse backgrounds/ages.

2.2. Top three benefits that REC brings?
2.2.1. Promoting English level;
2.2.2. Making/learning from friends with diverse background;
2.2.3. Sharing/learning new knowledge or concepts through discussions.

2.3. Conclusions and suggestions:
2.3.1. What most people love about REC are similar even if the orders are kind of different. Can we position REC as: The best English-learning platform to learn/share knowledge with friends in Taipei?

3. What should be improved?
3.1. All respondents think:
3.1.1. Quantity and diversity of participants;
3.1.2. English level;
3.1.3. Meeting enjoyment.

3.2. Regular members think:
3.2.1. Quantity and diversity of participants (55%);
3.2.2. English level;
3.2.3. Meeting enjoyment (32%), topics and questions, and quality of participants.

3.3. Disappeared members think:
3.3.1. English level (41.7%);
3.3.2. Quality, Quantity and diversity of participants;
3.3.3. Meeting enjoyment (29%);
3.3.4. 25% also mention the convenience of venue.

3.4. Female respondents concern “meeting enjoyment” improvement more than male ones.

3.5. English level is most important for age 31-50, and quantity and diversity of participants is most vital for age under 30.

3.6. Conclusions and suggestions:
3.6.1. Quantity and diversity of participants, English level, meeting enjoyment, quality of participants, topics and questions are the top five priorities we need to improve.
3.6.2. If we want to invite more disappeared members back, the convenience of venue also matters.
3.6.3. Female respondents concern “meeting enjoyment” more. If we want to have more female participants, how to make a meeting more fun and diversified is particularly vital.
3.6.4. English level and quantity and diversity of participants are the most important items to improve for the younger respondents. Therefore, if we wish to increase young people's participation, we need to especially improve them.

4. What kind of topics are more popular?
4.1. Top five popular topics for all respondents:
4.1.1. News and current events;
4.1.2. Self-development;
4.1.3. Communications and relationships;
4.1.4. New technologies;
4.1.5. Recreation and Travel.

4.2. Favorite topics by gender:
4.2.1. Top 5 are generally the same for both genders.
4.2.2. Top 2 for males are self-improvement and news and current events;
4.2.3. Top 2 for females are communications and relationships, and news and current events;
4.2.4. Males seem to like recreation and travel more.

4.3. Favorite topics by age:
4.3.1. Health ranks from No.6 to No.1 as age increases;
4.3.2. Communications and relationship is in Top 2 for age below 50;
4.3.3. Career development is more important for ages 23-50;
4.3.4. Self-improvement is greatly preferred for ages 23-40, and 50+.

4.4. Conclusions and suggestions:
4.4.1. News and current events, Self-development, Communications and relationships, New technologies, Recreation and Travel are the top five popular topics. If a host wish to have more participants, he/she can firstly choose these kinds of topics.
4.4.2. Females prefer communications and relationships and news and current events, so a host can focus on these two kinds of topics to attract more female participants.
4.4.3. Communications and relationship, Career development, and Self-improvement are preferred by the younger respondents, so a host can focus on these three kinds of topics to attract more young participants.

5. Other findings and suggestions:
5.1. REC has more than 400 members, with more than 200 of them in the line group, but we only received 55 responses. This means most members do not care much about REC. We need to find ways to increase members’ enthusiasm and adhesion to REC, so REC can be a life-long family of every member.

5.2. The key reason for members not coming back is Personal factors like job, family, venue, or time (61%), so it is not because of REC’s fault. However, 28% of them mention they chose to attend other clubs or gained little input from REC. We still need to pay attention to these problems.

5.3. Some non-member respondents mention that their reasons of still being non-members are because no one actively asks them to be a member, or they are still not familiar with REC members and system, so in the future the REC officers can be more active to introduce the REC to the guests and ask them to be members.


6. Other qualitative opinions
Besides previous quantitative responses, 22 respondents also provide valuable qualitative opinions, which can be probably our concrete ways or directions to improve. Those opinions are classified into five areas as follows.

6.1. Meeting quality and rules
6.1.1. 也可以用影片欣賞的方式進行。
6.1.2. 可做一些分組的競賽，如像藍白明對抗賽之類的嗎？或短劇演出，或是辨論等更多樣化的英文操練模式。
6.1.3. 除了線下聚會外，希望可以多一些線上的內容，讓無法參與聚會的會員可以有線上討論的空間。
6.1.4. 建議分組討論時，規定每人表達時間，如有不敢講的也必須要講（時間可縮短）。
6.1.5. 分桌討論時，讓新人以及英文程度稍弱者有機會表達而非僅是一兩位程度較高者滔滔不絕的講。
6.1.6. 討論時避免偏離主題，這樣可以讓有備而來的人不至於失望。
6.1.7. help new members and mentoring system
6.1.8. time control for presentation
6.1.9. 一桌只要一個人上台即可，太多人上台會讓聽眾失去興趣，也會壓縮分桌討論的時間。
6.1.10. I had an experience about time limit. A guest stood up to share what he think about the topic. He spoke for 8 minutes before I break his talk. If I did not say anything he would had spoken longer. That allowing a speaker to speak as long as he/she is willing to is not an appropriate idea. I think we still need to keep the limitation but how long it is can be discussed。
6.1.11. 擔心太多規則，恐讓多嘴者動則得咎，難以讓思維自由翱翔。但還是會盡量遵守規定，不會阻礙改革。

6.2. Topics and questions
6.2.1. 討論題目可以少一點，因時間不夠。
6.2.2. 每次會議討論的兩個主題同質性不要太高，不同的議題可以吸引不同的人參加 。
6.2.3. 盡量避免兩個主題都是影音，至少要有一篇是文章，文章可以增加字彙片語與及閱讀能力。
6.2.4. 所出的問題須與文章連結，開放性的問題為佳，可增進討論的熱絡度。
6.2.5. 文章和題目是我們的門面，也是一般人還沒來到我們的活動之前對我們的第一印象。一直以來，選擇的文章是很不錯的。但是下方的題目或是其他說明性文字往往讓人對我們的印象大打折扣了。
6.2.6. 當然，自己設計的題目但是對自己英文程度沒把握，如果有請native speaker作proofreading也是沒問題的。
6.2.7. 有些人是從某些學習和討論英語的網站取得文章和題目，這樣的題目就不會有怪怪的英文出現。
6.2.8. 多些活潑的內容。
6.2.9. 看到網站雖然有很多單元, 但是多是已有一段時間的活動資訊了，網站是網路世界的門牌，首頁就是最重要的門面，如果活動無法動態即時更新，那麼建議將每週四、六的會議主題調整到首頁最顯眼的位置，吸引訪客。
6.2.10. 如果每一次的會議主題，能夠有會員自己撰寫的文章，及熱心的會員評論主題的文章，那麼場子就會熱絡起來, 也會吸引訪客加入Royal的動機。

6.3. Participants
6.3.1. 若能增加年輕族群，更好。
6.3.2. 目前人變少了，雖是固定班底 但水平以下想提升英語文能力的人們 還是居多 這樣的基本上就會不敢去了 而有些則是太忙（但忙可能不是主要理由） 因為大家都太強了☺️ ，稍有些可惜 實為有些偏離原本REC 的初衷。
6.3.3 多邀朋友參加。
6.3.4. 我不知道吸收外國人是不是重要的事，我想跟大家討論這件事的利弊。
6.3.5. REC 活躍成員以年長者居多，不知是否Toastmasters 的活躍成員結構較為年輕，如果是，我們可以參考並思考吸引年輕族群的誘因為何。
6.3.6. 由於退休人士與自由業偏多, 而上班族或年輕人偏少, 在討論議題時, 較容易受限。

6.4. Venue
6.4.1. 可否在小樹屋舉行，不受干擾。
6.4.2. 從上班地點去西門, 再回家, 不順利, 再加上停車不方便。
6.4.3. 地點能夠變動，使場所有新鮮感，且有比較那個地點最好，如：通風、空間足、廁所乾淨衛生、時間有些逾時，也不趕人，等等。
6.4.4. 可多出去玩。

6.5. Encouragement
6.5.1. Royal 最棒，加油，加油。
6.5.2. Gogo!
6.5.3. Joined efforts make a difference
6.5.4. Good for now
6.5.5. REC的氣氛溫和, 有些成員蠻親切的。
6.5.6. 謝謝，辛苦了。
6.5.7. 每次聚會都很溫馨，可暢所欲言。
6.5.8. 感謝您的用心。這樣的問卷對我們club會是很有幫助的！
6.5.9. 會員看起很多退休公務人員，素質很高，發言的機會也足夠，這滿足了加入club想要切磋英語交談的需求。

Questions:
1. According to the survey, the younger respondents think "English level" and "quantity and diversity of participants" are the most important ones to improve. What might be the reasons that younger respondents think so? How can we concretely improve our English level and quantity and diversity of participants?

2. According to the survey, it is interesting that females seem to prefer a topic on “communications and relationships” more, while males prefer “self-improvement”. What are the possible reasons for the difference?

3. 22 respondents provide valuable qualitative opinions in five areas. Which opinions do you also agree with or you consider valuable/constructive to REC? What can we do to put them into practice?

4. REC has more than 400 members with more than 200 of them in the line group, but we only received 55 responses. This means most members do not care much about REC. What can we do to increase members’ adhesion and enthusiasm to REC, so REC can be a life-long family of every member?

5. According to what most respondents love about REC, can we have a slogan of REC as: The best English-learning platform to learn/share knowledge with friends in Taipei? Any better suggestions/ideas?

6. How do you generally think about this questionnaire survey? Are the results or suggestions helpful or useful to REC? Why or why not?


Session II: Elon Musk’s Top 4 Mind-blowing Predictions for 2021

1. Fully Autonomous Consumer Vehicles Will Arrive;

2. Satellite Internet Available to the Entire Inhabited World;

3. Humans will Implant Microchips into Their Brain to Help Cure Illness;

4. Solar power will explode.

Please read or watch the following article and video for more details:
https://medium.com/the-innovation/elon- ... 2fbf7daaad

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8_-yYrXNft4

Questions: 　
1. How much do you know about Elon Musk? Do you like him? Why or why not?

2. Among the four predictions, which one is the most mind-blowing to you? Which one is most likely/unlikely to happen in 2021? Why?

3. Will you take Autonomous Consumer Vehicles if they are available in 2021? When will be better time to take it?

4. Will you accept implanting microchips into your brain to help cure illness? Why or why not?
