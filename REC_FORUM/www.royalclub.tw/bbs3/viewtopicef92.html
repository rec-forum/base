Session I: 7 Things You Should Give Up If You Want to Be Happy
https://medium.com/swlh/7-things-you-should-give-up-if-you-want-to-be-happy-a74994c0bd48

“It isn’t what you have or who you are or where you are or what you are doing that makes you happy or unhappy. It is what you think about it.” ― Dale Carnegie

Let’s face it, we all want to be happy.

If you didn’t want to be happy you wouldn’t be reading this.

You talk about how you want to be happy, but you don’t ever seem to do anything about it.

If you want to be happy you don’t need to add more things to our life — you need to give some up.

Here is a list of things that you need to give up to create more happiness in your life.

1. Give Up On Your Fears
“Where fear is, happiness is not.” — Lucius Annaeus Seneca

2. Give Up On Your Negative Self-talk
“Positive thinking and negative thinking cannot operate at the same level in your mind, one needs to be the master and the one you feed it more will rule over the other.” ― Oscar Bimpong

3. Give Up On Your Past
“Just because someone screwed up your past, it doesn’t mean you should give them permission to screw up your future.” — Zig Ziglar

4. Give Up On Living Other People’s Dreams
“The two most important days in your life are the day you are born and the day you find out why.” — Mark Twain

5. Give Up On Seeking the Approval of Others
“An amazing thing happens when you stop seeking approval and validation: You find it. People are naturally drawn like magnets to those who know who they are and cannot be shaken!”- Mandy Hale

6. Give Up Comparing Yourself to Others
“No one can make you feel inferior without your consent.” — Eleanor Roosevelt

7. Give Up Negative People
“You cannot expect to live a positive life if you hang with negative people.” ― Joel Osteen

Conclusion:
“Remember: We all get what we tolerate. So stop tolerating excuses within yourself, limiting beliefs of the past, or half-assed (= stupid) or fearful states.” ―Tony Robbins

Happiness is a choice.

You are in control of your happiness.

Every day you get to choose to be happy or not.

Quit making excuses.

Quit blaming others.

Quit blaming your circumstances.

It’s time to take responsibility for your own happiness.

Discussion questions:

1. Do you agree with the notion that If you want to be happy you don’t need to add more things to your life — you need to give some up? Why or why not?

2. Among the 7 things you should give up to be happy, which one or ones would be on your priority list? Why?

3. Are there any other things that you want to give up to make you happy?

4. What are the common traits of a happy person?

5. What’s your secrets to be a happy person?


Session II: Jeff Bezos Says This 1 Sentence Is the Key to Amazon’s Success
http://customerthink.com/the-14-leadership-principles-that-drive-amazon/
By Scott Mautz

Bezos has been sharing one powerful sentence of advice, over and over again, for the past 20 years.

It communicates the key to Amazon’s success, according to Bezos:

“Setting the bar high in our approach to hiring has been, and will continue to be, the single most important element of Amazon.com’s success.”

Amazon most certainly takes its hiring process seriously. A key part of the process is to ask questions that assess how well each candidate scores on Amazon’s 14 Leadership Principles. I’ve listed the principles below, edited for brevity:

1. Customer obsession
Leaders start with the customer and work backward. They pay attention to competitors but obsess over customers.

2. Ownership
Leaders are owners. They act beyond just their own team and never say “that’s not my job.”

3. Invent and simplify
Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams, and always find ways to simplify.

4. Are right, a lot
Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts.

5. Learn and be curious
Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They’re curious.

6. Hire and develop the best
They recognize exceptional talent and willingly move them throughout the organization. Leaders develop leaders and take seriously their role in coaching others.

7. Insist on the highest standards
Leaders are continually raising the bar and driving their teams to deliver high-quality products, services, and processes.

8. Think big
Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results.

9. Bias for action
Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study. We value calculated risk taking.

10. Frugality
Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention.

11. Earn trust
Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully.

12. Dive deep
Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ.

13. Have backbone; disagree and commit
Leaders have conviction and are tenacious. They do not compromise for the sake of social cohesion.

14. Deliver results
Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.

Discussion questions:

1. Why set the bar high so important to Amazon’s success when it comes to its hiring process?

2. Is setting the bar high a good thing or bad thing when it comes to your life goals or dealing with important things?

3. If you were the CEO of a company, which ones of these 14 leadership principles do you think would be the most important factors to your company’s success? Why?

4. In your opinion, what are the best qualities of a great leader?

5. You are the CEO of your life. Do you think you can apply some of these 14 leadership principles to your life? Please share your thoughts?

6. Are there any words of wisdom or quotes from famous people (or someone you admire) that have ever inspired you?
