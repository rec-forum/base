Session 2
An Outing Diary

April 24, 2021 Saturday Sunny
This morning I went to 鶯歌by MRT and train. Because it’s the 1st time for me to ride a train to 鶯歌, I missed the train that Royal English club members planned to get . Fortunately, I asked a kind and right person to get the correct shuttle train at right side and right time. Louie asked 4 late persons- I’m included to add his WECHAT but I don’t register WECHAT. He said he could find us after we went off the train because WECHAT offers an instant position option.
But I don’t know about WECHAT, I posted a photo to line to confirm which car I am at. Luckily Crystal found me . What a relief!


After we got off the train, I followed Crystal to move towards the English tour guide- Mike. He said he’ll introduce us the area by English as much as possible but still use some Chinese to explain some difficult terminologies.
We are a group of English maniacs! Last year during the pandemic we used zoom meeting to practice English. Today we practiced English all the way. Look at group pictures, some are over 70 years old. “ it’s never too late to learn.”They're good models for us to learn.


鶯歌was named after “ parrot “ because parrot was pronounced to 鸚哥in Taiwanese. There’s a huge stone in 鶯歌whose shape looks like a parrot. When 鄭成功’s troops passed by the stone, they got sick because of miasma around the stone. 鄭成功ordered his soldiers fired the stone by cannon to break its neck, so only the parrot’s body left to now.
鶯歌 is famous for its ceramics. We visited many stores concerning chinaware and pottery. We also saw an iconic symbol- parrot statue standing in there. There are some ruins of tunnel kilns existing but the guide said they are more likely to be
demolished soon. Hence we can be the last group to witness their existences. Then I thought of 福島 in Japan, if you had sightseed福島, you would have felt lucky after earthquake and tsunami destroyed 福島.
People reach some age, we begin to miss those happy times. Nostalgia feeling comes like a long-lost friend. I found it in the movie, in the novel, or even an old photo.


An old building is standing in my memory. The old diary comes alive when I open it. My past handwriting, yellowing paper, and moldy smell are reminding me: contents could bring me back decades ago to seek the old building.
Questions for discussion:

3. Did you ever open your past diaries to review/ seek the sweet/ unforgettable moment?
