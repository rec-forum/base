Session 1: How do children choose a best friend?

https://www.bbc.com/future/article/2021 ... est-friend

Q1.Do you think you are the person who is easy to make friends? What kinds of personalities or characteristics do you have? What do your friends think of you? (nice/easy going/stubborn/reliable…)

Q2.When you want to choose a friend, what are the elements that you concern? (personal/easy going/the same common…)

Q3.Do you still keep in touch with your kindergarten/elementary/college… friends? How do you keep your friendship for so many years? How long do you have a gathering?

Q4.Do you agree with the benefits of the article of best friends? What do you learn from your best friend?

Q5.If there is the school reunion, will you go? Why or why not? Anything you want to say to your schoolmates/old friends if you meet them?

Session 2: How To Never Run Out Of Things To Say In Conversation

https://tw.voicetube.com/videos/61241

Q1.Are you the person who is shy or open-minded? Do you usually wait for someone to talk with you first or you start a conversation first in a new group?

Q2.How do you usually break the ice when you talk to a new friend in a group? What kinds of topics will you start? How do you do for helping new comers to involve more in Royal English club?

Q3.Do you have any friend who is very easy to talk with? What personality do they have? Have you ever learned the tips from them?

Q4.What are the suitable or forbidden topics in a conversation? Why? If someone always talk about the forbidden topics in the groups, what will you do?

Q5.Do you still remember the first time when you speak English in public/on the stage/in Royal English club? How did you feel or overcome the fear? Any tip to share with us?
